--- layout: post title: yes(1) tags: - command line ninja magic - computer science - 'yes' status: publish type: post published: true meta: _edit_last: '2' --- Yes, yes(1) is built-in to Mac and Linux (at least OS X Lion and Ubuntu 11.04). And, as you might guess, it repeatedly prints a string of your choice ('y' by default) followed by a newline to stdout. Its sole purpose in life is to automate agreeing to prompts. I encountered it recently in a script that was automating RAID array deployment on EC2 ephemeral disks: # mdadm doesn't let you automate by default, so pipe in 'y'! yes | mdadm ...
